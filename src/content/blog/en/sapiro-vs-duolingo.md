SAPIRO and Duolingo are both learning apps, but on different territory. Duolingo teaches languages. SAPIRO develops general knowledge. Here is where they overlap and diverge.

## What you learn

**Duolingo.** Language learning: English, Spanish, German, Italian, Mandarin, etc. Over 40 languages available. Linguistic skill: vocabulary, grammar, conjugation, listening, expression.

**SAPIRO.** General knowledge learning: geography (197 countries, flags, capitals), history (500+ figures), art (553 works), nature (600 animal species). Factual and cultural skill.

## Method

**Duolingo.** Heavy gamification: short lessons, badges, hearts, streaks, friend leaderboards. Very addictive. Streak pressure.

**SAPIRO.** Pure quiz format with educational explanation. More pedagogical, less addictive. No aggressive notifications. See our article on [gamification of learning](/en/blog/gamification-education/).

## Free tier

**Duolingo.** Free with ads and heart limit (long recharge after errors). Super Duolingo ($8-14/month) removes that.

**SAPIRO.** Free with all features, no ads, no limits. Sapiro+ ($1.99/month or $39.99 lifetime) for extended features.

## Advertising

**Duolingo.** Lots of ads in the free version. Can be heavy.

**SAPIRO.** Zero ads. See our article on [best ad-free educational apps](/en/blog/best-ad-free-educational-apps/).

## Data collection

**Duolingo.** Significant data collection for personalization and advertising. Mandatory account.

**SAPIRO.** No data collection. No mandatory account. See our article on [quiz apps without data collection](/en/blog/quiz-apps-no-data-collection/).

## Target audience

**Duolingo.** Language learners, all levels. Adults wanting to travel, students alongside their classes, professionals.

**SAPIRO.** Curious of all ages. Families, students, exam candidates, adults seeking general culture.

## When to use each

**Duolingo if** you want to learn a language from scratch or consolidate. Progressive, structured, playful but repetitive.

**SAPIRO if** you want to develop your general knowledge (countries, history, art, nature). Educational quiz learning.

## Can they coexist?

Yes. Many users combine both: 15 minutes of language in the morning with Duolingo, 15 minutes of general knowledge in the evening with SAPIRO. They are complementary skills, not competitors.
