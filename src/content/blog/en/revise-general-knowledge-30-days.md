Thirty days to revise general knowledge is short but realistic if done well. Here is a day-by-day plan to cover the essentials.

## Week 1: history and institutions

**Day 1.** Antiquity, Middle Ages, Renaissance. See our [synthesis of the three major periods](/en/blog/antiquity-middle-ages-renaissance-overview/).

**Day 2.** 17th-18th centuries. Absolute monarchies, Enlightenment, American Revolution, French Revolution.

**Day 3.** 19th century. Empires, Restoration, Belle Epoque, colonization.

**Day 4.** 20th century: world wars. See our article on [the great battles of history](/en/blog/great-battles-history/).

**Day 5.** 20th century: Cold War and decolonization. See our article on [20th century dictators](/en/blog/20th-century-dictators/).

**Day 6.** Domestic institutions. Constitution, separation of powers, territorial organization.

**Day 7.** International institutions. UN, EU, NATO, WTO.

## Week 2: geography and economics

**Day 8.** National geography. Regions, rivers, climates, demographics.

**Day 9.** European geography. Countries, capitals, mountains, seas.

**Day 10.** World geography. Continents, countries by GDP, world demographics.

**Day 11.** Economic geography. Globalization, flows, powers, BRICS.

**Day 12.** National economy. GDP, unemployment, inflation, debt, state budget.

**Day 13.** Sociology. Demographics, immigration, aging, urbanization.

**Day 14.** Mock quiz weeks 1-2 to check progress.

## Week 3: science and arts

**Day 15.** Fundamental sciences. Evolution, genetics, atom, energy.

**Day 16.** Climate sciences. Greenhouse effect, IPCC, agreements, energy transition.

**Day 17.** Digital. Internet, AI, data, cybersecurity.

**Day 18.** Art history. See our articles on [art movements](/en/blog/art-movements-explained/) and [famous paintings](/en/blog/famous-paintings-masterpieces/).

**Day 19.** Literature. Major authors by century.

**Day 20.** Music and cinema. Major composers, must-know directors.

**Day 21.** Heritage. National monuments, UNESCO sites.

## Week 4: current events and revisions

**Day 22.** Political events of the last 12 months.

**Day 23.** Recent economic events.

**Day 24.** International events (wars, elections, treaties).

**Day 25.** Scientific and environmental events.

**Day 26.** Full mock quiz to measure progress.

**Day 27.** Review mistakes from previous quizzes.

**Day 28.** Full exam simulation in real conditions.

**Day 29.** Relative rest. Notes review only.

**Day 30.** Day before exam. Relaxed reading, early sleep.

## Daily method

**1.5 hours per day.** 30 minutes reading (press + textbook chapter). 30 minutes notes (creating or reviewing). 30 minutes quizzes.

**Quizzes as main tool.** SAPIRO offers 2,000+ general knowledge questions perfect for these 30-minute sessions. [The science of memorization](/en/blog/gamification-education/) shows quizzes multiply learning efficiency.

**Mistakes notebook.** Write each mistake in a notebook to review three days later. Spaced repetition principle.

**Sleep and exercise.** Seven to eight hours of sleep minimum. Thirty minutes of exercise daily. The brain consolidates during sleep.

See also our [full general knowledge guide](/en/blog/general-knowledge-guide/) for the long-term method.
