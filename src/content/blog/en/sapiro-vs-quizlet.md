SAPIRO and Quizlet are both learning apps, but they do different things. Quizlet is a flashcard tool for personalized revision. SAPIRO is a pre-built general knowledge quiz. Here are the key differences to choose.

## Philosophy

**Quizlet.** The user creates their own flashcards (vocabulary, dates, formulas). Ideal for revising a specific course. Test mode, game mode, listening mode.

**SAPIRO.** The user plays pre-built quizzes on geography, history, art, nature. 2,000+ verified questions with educational explanations. No personal creation.

## Content

**Quizlet.** Community library of millions of flashcard sets created by users. Quality varies widely. Lots of school content: language vocabulary, biology, law, medicine.

**SAPIRO.** 100% editorially verified content. Four universes: 197 countries (geography), 500+ historical figures, 553 art works, 600 animal species. No user-generated content.

## Free tier

**Quizlet.** Free for basic features. Quizlet Plus ($4/month) for advanced features (ChatGPT for question generation, offline modes, statistics).

**SAPIRO.** Free with all essential features, no ads, no data collection. Sapiro+ ($1.99/month, $19.99/year or $39.99 lifetime) for extended features (detailed stats, special modes).

## Advertising

**Quizlet.** Ads in the free version, removed with Plus.

**SAPIRO.** No ads, even in the free version. See our articles on [best ad-free educational apps](/en/blog/best-ad-free-educational-apps/).

## Data

**Quizlet.** Collects user data for personalization and advertising. Standard digital app policy.

**SAPIRO.** No data collection. No mandatory account, no tracking cookies, no sharing with third parties. See our article on [quiz apps without data collection](/en/blog/quiz-apps-no-data-collection/).

## Target audience

**Quizlet.** Students, professionals revising a specific course. Cramming and spaced repetition tool.

**SAPIRO.** Any curious audience: adults seeking general culture, families, students of all levels, professionals studying for exams.

## Learning experience

**Quizlet.** Flashcard mode, test mode, listening mode, "Quizlet Live" multiplayer for class. Very complete for repetition.

**SAPIRO.** Pure quiz format with contextualized explanation behind each question. More educational for general knowledge.

## Verdict: when to choose each

**Choose Quizlet if** you want to revise a specific course (English vocabulary, anatomy, law) by creating your own flashcards.

**Choose SAPIRO if** you want to develop your general culture, alone or with family, with quality pre-built content and zero ads.
